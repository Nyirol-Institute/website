Volunteer With Us

Share your skills and make a lasting impact by helping South Sudanese youth build careers in technology.

Why Volunteer With Us

Volunteering with Nyirol Institute is a unique opportunity to make a meaningful difference in the lives of South Sudanese youth while sharing your skills and expertise. Our volunteers play a crucial role in our mission to provide quality technology education and create pathways out of poverty.

Whether you're a tech professional, educator, or someone passionate about making a difference, your contribution can help transform lives and communities. Volunteers bring diverse perspectives, specialized knowledge, and additional capacity that enhances our programs and expands our impact.

Join our global community of volunteers who are helping to build a brighter future for South Sudan through technology education and innovation.

Volunteer Opportunities

We offer a variety of volunteer opportunities that match different skills, interests, and time commitments. Whether you can volunteer remotely or in person, for a few hours or several months, we have options that will allow you to make a meaningful contribution.

Technical Instructor

Share your technical expertise by teaching classes or workshops in areas such as programming, web development, graphic design, data analysis, or other tech specializations.

Time Commitment: Flexible, from one-time workshops to regular classes

Location: In-person or remote

Career Mentor

Guide students in their professional development through one-on-one mentoring, resume reviews, interview preparation, and career advice in technology fields.

Time Commitment: 1-2 hours per week for 3+ months

Location: Remote (video calls)

Project Coach

Support student teams working on tech projects by providing guidance, feedback, and technical assistance throughout the development process.

Time Commitment: 2-4 hours per week for project duration

Location: Remote or in-person

Solar Technology Specialist

Help maintain and optimize our solar power systems, train staff on maintenance procedures, and support the expansion of our sustainable energy infrastructure.

Time Commitment: 1-4 weeks on-site

Location: In-person

IT Support

Assist with maintaining our computer labs, troubleshooting technical issues, setting up networks, and implementing IT solutions for our educational programs.

Time Commitment: Flexible, from remote assistance to on-site support

Location: Remote or in-person

Curriculum Developer

Help create or improve educational materials, lesson plans, and learning resources for our technology programs, ensuring they are relevant and effective.

Time Commitment: Flexible, project-based

Location: Remote

Volunteer Stories

Michael Johnson

Web Development Instructor | Remote Volunteer from Canada

"I've been volunteering as a remote web development instructor for Nyirol Institute for the past year, teaching weekly classes via video conference. It's been incredibly rewarding to see the progress of my students, many of whom had never written a line of code before our first class.

What I love most about volunteering with Nyirol Institute is the direct impact I can see. One of my students recently landed a remote job as a junior developer, which will completely transform his life and his family's future. The dedication and enthusiasm of the students is truly inspiring, and it motivates me to continue sharing my knowledge and skills.

Even though I volunteer remotely, I feel deeply connected to the community and mission of Nyirol Institute. The staff provides excellent support, and I've built meaningful relationships with both colleagues and students."

Sophia Chen

UX Design Mentor | On-site Volunteer for 3 months

"I took a sabbatical from my job as a UX designer to spend three months on-site at Nyirol Institute, and it was one of the most transformative experiences of my life. I worked with a group of talented students in the UX/UI Design specialization, mentoring them through real-world projects and helping them build professional portfolios.

Living and working in Nyirol County gave me a deep appreciation for the challenges and opportunities in the region. The resilience and creativity of the students continually amazed me, and I learned as much from them as they did from me. We tackled projects focused on local needs, designing interfaces for solar energy monitoring, agricultural information systems, and educational tools.

The experience has changed my perspective on design, technology, and education. I've returned to my job with new insights and a continued connection to Nyirol Institute, where I still mentor students remotely and help connect graduates with opportunities in my professional network."

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to have teaching experience to volunteer?

No, teaching experience is not required for all volunteer roles. While it's beneficial for instructional positions, we provide guidance and support to help you succeed. Your technical expertise and willingness to share knowledge are the most important qualifications.

What is the time commitment for volunteers?

We offer flexible volunteer opportunities to accommodate different schedules. Commitments range from one-time workshops to regular weekly sessions or longer-term placements. During the application process, we'll work with you to find an arrangement that fits your availability.

Can I volunteer remotely?

Yes, many of our volunteer positions can be done remotely, including technical instruction, mentoring, curriculum development, and some IT support roles. Remote volunteers connect with our students and staff through video conferencing and collaborative online tools.

What support will I receive as a volunteer?

All volunteers receive an orientation to our programs and community, ongoing support from our staff, access to teaching resources and materials, and regular check-ins to address any questions or challenges. For in-person volunteers, we also provide accommodation arrangements and local orientation.

What is the application and selection process?

After submitting your application, our volunteer coordinator will review it and contact you for an initial interview. If there's a good match between your skills and our needs, we'll conduct a more detailed discussion about specific opportunities and arrangements. The entire process typically takes 2-3 weeks.
